Biography

Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary and political leader. He served as President of South Africa from 1994 to 1999 and was the country's first black head of state and the first elected in a fully representative democratic election. His presidency marked the end of decades of institutionalized racial discrimination and inspired hope for a new era of reconciliation and equality.
