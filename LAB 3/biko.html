Steve Biko

Anti-Apartheid Activist and Visionary Leader

About Steve Biko

Steve Biko, born on December 18, 1946, was a prominent anti-apartheid activist and a key figure in the Black Consciousness Movement in South Africa.

His tireless efforts aimed to empower black South Africans, instilling pride in their identity and challenging the oppressive apartheid regime.

Notable Achievements

  • Co-founder of the South African Students' Organisation (SASO)
  • Leader of the Black Consciousness Movement
  • Author of "I Write What I Like," a collection of his writings

Legacy

Steve Biko's legacy continues to inspire generations. His commitment to justice and equality left an indelible mark on the struggle against apartheid.

Though he tragically died in police custody on September 12, 1977, Biko's ideas and principles live on, influencing movements for human rights globally.

Memorable Quotes

"The most potent weapon in the hands of the oppressor is the mind of the oppressed."

"Black man, you are on your own."
